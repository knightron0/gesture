how it works

Gesture uses machine learning to interpret ASL fingerspelling. This interactive learning platform allows users to test the accuracy of their fingerspelling skills and shows demonstrations of proper ASL fingerspelling.

Why is gesture important?

01.

MEDICAL EMERGENCIES

If you or someone around you is experiencing a medical emergency and is unable to communicate verbally, being able to interpret ASL can help quickly convey important information about the person's condition, allergies, medications, and any other relevant details.

02.

NATURAL DISASTERS

In the event of a natural disaster, such as an earthquake, hurricane, or tornado, communication infrastructure can be severely impacted, making it difficult to communicate through traditional means. Knowing ASL can help you communicate with deaf or hard-of-hearing individuals who may not be able to hear emergency announcements or instructions.

03.

FIRE EMERGENCIES

In a fire emergency, smoke and noise can make it difficult to hear or speak. Knowing ASL can help you communicate with anyone who may be deaf or hard-of-hearing and ensure that everyone is aware of the emergency and can evacuate safely.

04.

LAW ENFORCEMENT ENCOUNTERS

If you are a law enforcement officer, knowing ASL can be helpful in communicating with deaf or hard-of-hearing individuals who may be involved in a traffic stop or other encounter.

In all of these situations, being able to interpret ASL can help bridge the communication gap and ensure that everyone involved is aware of the emergency and can take appropriate action.
